  [1X3.1 [33X[0;0YIntroduction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  a  semisimple category [23XC[123X of the form [23X\bigoplus_{i \in I} k\mathrm{-vec}[123X
  to become a rigid symmetric closed monoidal skeletal category, the set [23XI[123X has
  to be equipped with extra strucutre. To become a skeletal category, we need:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ya total ordering on [23XI[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo become a monoidal category, we need:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ya  function  [23X\texttt{IsYieldingIdentities}[123X, deciding whether an object
        yields  the  identity whenever it is part of an associator triple or a
        braiding pair,[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Yfunctions  [23X\texttt{Multiplicity}[123X  and  [23X\texttt{*}[123X, defining the tensor
        product on objects,[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ya function [23X\texttt{AssociatorFromData}[123X, defining the tensor product on
        morphisms.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo become a symmetric monoidal category, we need:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ya function [23X\texttt{ExteriorPower}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo become a rigid symmetric monoidal category, we need:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ya function [23X\texttt{Dual}[123X, defining duals on objects.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn the following, two families of such sets [23XI[123X are introduced:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[23X\texttt{GIrreducibleObject}[123X:  For a group [23XG[123X, the set [23XI[123X consists of the
        irreducible   characters   of  [23XG[123X.  We  call  the  elements  in  [23XI[123X  the
        [23XG[123X-irreducible objects.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[23X\texttt{GZGradedIrreducibleObject}[123X:  For a group [23XG[123X, the set [23XI[123X consists
        of  the  irreducible  characters  of  [23XG[123X  together  with a degree [23Xn \in
        \mathbb{Z}[123X.  We  call  the  elements in [23XI[123X the [23XG-\mathbb{Z}[123X-irreducible
        objects.[133X
